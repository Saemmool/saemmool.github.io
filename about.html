    Saemmool Lee is a multimedia journalist and published author based in Seoul, South Korea.

    She started her career as a beat reporter at the Dong-A Ilbo, one of the biggest and oldest daily newspapers in Seoul, covering a wide range of beats from crime to business for six years. Then she earned her master's degree from the UC Berkeley Graduate School of Journalism('19) with a concentration in multimedia. During her graduate studies, she worked at the Center for Information Technology Research in the Interest of Society (CITRIS) and the Banatao Institute communications team, where she produced various content about startups, technologies and research initiatives.

    Currently, she is leading the Dong-A Ilbo's flagship multimedia projects. Her works include "Jeungbal(Evaporation)", which won the Grand Prize of the "Digital Journalism Awards" in Korea as well as the "Journalist of the Month Award" by the Journalist Association of Korea.

    She has authored four books, and published numerous stories which you can find here.

Part 1. When I was Young

When I was seven, my dad asked me to get a bottle of water from a well in our neighborhood. I went to the well and tried to fill water to my bottle - but the water was too far from my short arms. I slowly put myself into the well so that my bottle get close to water, but suddenly I fell into the well.

I thought I was dying that day. But then my friend's dad who was passing by ran into and rescued me. That was the first experience of facing that, that I remember.

I faced death several times since I was young. When I was in high school, I got gas poioning from coal briquettes while sleeping. I lost my consciousness, and brought to a hospital. Since then, I seriously thought about my life, spirit and death.

Part 2. What if I Disappear Eternally?

It was scary to face death alone in unexpected moment. I was filled with anguish, because I didn't know what would happen to my spirit after death. I read many religious books and listened to the speeches from religious leaders expecting that they would give me an answer. However, those did not provide any answers to me.

What if death comes to me all of a sudden, though I worked so hard for my life? What if I disappear eternally from the whole human history? If I leave this world in my 10s or 20s by accident, what does my life mean that I lived until then? Who would care if such a small man disappears? People in this world woud be busy for their lives, and the lives would go on.

Then I was learning Bible two hours a week attending a mission school. According to Bible, the death came in due to our sins. There, my concern began: I couldn't deny that I had sins, though I didn't believe in Bible or Jesus at that time. What if the words in Bible are truth? What if I fall into the hell after I die, even I didn't believe in the world after death?

Part 3. The Biggest Issue in Our Lives

There was nothing I can predict about my life but death. Fortunately, everyone knows that we will die someday. There is a saying that the four sufferings of life are 'being born, getting old, getting sick, and dying.' Among them, the biggest issue was death.

If death is the end of life, we are living our lives with pre-destined death. We departs to our deaths by the time we are born. We are death-sentenced prisoners, waiting for the execution inside of a big prison called 'the world.' As we live our lives, we are getting close to our death.

Living is dying, if we look at the otherside. No one wants to die, but no one can avoid dying. Even if someone insists that he or she is not afraid of dying, fear of dying and disappearance remains deep inside of the person's heart. Some people deny this, and try to run away from death as much as they can. However, our lives co-exists with death all the time.

A tombstones of catholic priests in Daegu says'Hodi Mihi Cras Tibi' . In translation, "Today Me, Tomorrow You." It means, 'I'm lying here today, but your turn is tomorrow.' The message is, you'll need to think about and prepare death while living.

Part 4. Is Death an End to Everything?

While I've been thinking about death for long, I realized several facts.

I didn't know whether the death is the ultimate end or if there is another world. It would be so vein if I disappear eternally by the time I die. What if there is a world beyond death as many religions say, and also there are trials and punishments? I was not sure if I can go to a good place or would be put into suffering place after the trial.

In statistics, almost 85 percent of population globally have religions. Every religion has three common things: First, flesh is not the essence of human being. There is a spirit and it is much important than flesh. Second, this world is not everything and there is a life after death. Third, there will be trials and punishments after death.

Death takes away everything. It takes up all, including our ego, pride, and honor. Death makes us let things go and empty ourselves, which are the things we cannot do by ourselves. Death makes us to think about our lives seriously, and be humble.

Part 5. Memento Mori

Fyodor Dostoevsky wrote to his brother:
“When I look back at the past and think of all the time I squandered in error and idleness, lacking the knowledge I needed to live; when I think of how I sinned against my heart and my soul, then my heart bleeds. Life is a gift, life is happiness … Every minute could have been an eternity of happiness! If youth only knew. Now my life will change, now I will be reborn.”

"Memento mori" means "remember that you die" in Latin. In the old days, a victorious general would have someoone standing behind him, whispering such words and emphasize to "remember you're only a man."

If there's an eternal world that our spirits go after deaths, this would be the most important matter in our lives. If you find an answer to that, you would be very lucky person - like me as of now.
